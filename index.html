What is the SACE?

The South Australian Certificate of Education (SACE) is the secondary school qualification for students in South Australia, typically awarded at the end of Year 12. It is recognized nationwide and is essential for gaining entry into universities, colleges, and other higher education institutions across the country.

Students working toward their SACE must complete a variety of courses and assessments that cover a range of subjects, including English, mathematics, sciences, humanities, and the arts. The SACE system is flexible, allowing students to choose from a wide range of subjects based on their interests and career aspirations.

What is the ATAR?

The Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR) is a numerical ranking given to students upon the completion of their secondary education, which is used by universities to assess eligibility for entry. The ATAR score is calculated using a student's best subject results, which are then scaled and weighted to create a rank from 0.00 to 99.95.

In South Australia, the ATAR is calculated based on the results of the SACE and can be used for university applications. It is important to note that the ATAR is a relative ranking, meaning it is based on a student's performance compared to others in their cohort. The higher the ATAR, the more competitive the student’s application is for university entry.

How is the ATAR Calculated?

The ATAR is calculated using the best 90 credits from a student’s SACE. These credits include both school assessments and external examinations, and are assigned a certain score based on performance. The ATAR is calculated by the South Australian Tertiary Admissions Centre (SATAC) and is typically released after final exams in December.

Why is the ATAR Important?

The ATAR is a critical factor for students wishing to pursue higher education. It helps determine eligibility for courses at universities and other tertiary institutions across Australia. While the ATAR is not the only consideration for entry, it is one of the key criteria used by many universities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the SACE and ATAR systems play an essential role in the education journey of students in South Australia. By understanding how the SACE works and how the ATAR is calculated, students can make informed decisions about their studies and future educational pathways.
